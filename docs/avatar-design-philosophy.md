# Kawaii Gourmand — Guest Avatar Design Philosophy

## The Movement

**Kawaii Gourmand** celebrates the intersection of Japanese-inspired cuteness (kawaii) with Vietnamese culinary warmth. Each avatar is a tiny, lovable food spirit — round, soft, and irresistibly friendly — designed to make guests feel welcomed the moment they sit down, even before ordering.

## Form & Space

Softness is non-negotiable. Every shape begins with a circle or a perfectly rounded rectangle. Corners are generous, edges are absent. The characters occupy their space with a gentle confidence — centered, balanced, neither crowded nor floating. Each avatar is a self-contained world: a single character on a clean, transparent background, perfectly sized to fit a 48px circle while remaining legible at 24px. The proportions follow chibi rules — oversized heads (60-70% of total height), tiny bodies, stubby limbs that suggest movement without motion. Eyes are simple: two small dots or gentle arcs, positioned low on the face to maximize the forehead and amplify cuteness. Mouths are minimal — a small curve, a tiny "w" shape, or nothing at all. Expression lives in the eyes.

## Color & Material

The palette draws from the warm, appetizing spectrum of Vietnamese cuisine: soft peach (#FECACA), warm amber (#FDE68A), creamy matcha (#BBF7D0), gentle taro purple (#DDD6FE), and blushing lychee pink (#FBCFE8). Each character gets one dominant color that defines its personality. Shading is flat with a single shadow tone — no gradients, no textures. The aesthetic is clean, vector-crisp, and meticulously crafted with the precision of a master illustrator who has spent years perfecting the economy of line. A subtle blush (two small pink circles on the cheeks) is the universal mark of warmth across all characters, applied with painstaking consistency.

## Scale & Rhythm

The set operates as a family. While each character has a unique identity (a different food spirit — a bowl of phở, a bánh mì, a cup of trà đá, a rice ball, a dumpling), they share identical proportions, line weight, eye style, and blush placement. This rhythmic consistency is the product of deep expertise — it takes countless hours to make variety feel unified. When six avatars sit side by side, they should read as siblings, not strangers. The visual rhythm creates a sense of community: you're not alone at this table, you're part of a charming little food family.

## Composition & Balance

Each avatar is designed for circular clipping — every element must live comfortably within a perfect circle. No detail extends beyond the safe zone. The character is vertically centered with slight bottom-weighting (the "seated" feeling). Small accessories float naturally: a tiny leaf on top, a wisp of steam, a small sparkle. These accents are placed with the precision of a jeweler — each one deliberate, each one adding character without adding noise. The final execution must appear as though someone at the absolute top of their field labored over every curve, every pixel, every proportion with the care of a master craftsman creating heirloom miniatures.
